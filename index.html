Thai Mango Sticky Rice Recipe

Mango sticky rice is a popular Thai dessert made from sweet sticky rice, fresh mango slices, and a rich coconut milk sauce. It is known for its delightful combination of sweet and salty flavors, often garnished with toasted sesame seeds or crispy mung beans.

Ingredients

    Coconut Sticky Rice
  • 1 cup white glutinous rice
  • ⅔ cup coconut milk
  • ½ teaspoon table salt
  • ½ cup sugar
    • Salted Coconut Sauce
  • ½ cup coconut milk
  • ¼ teaspoon table salt
  • 1 teaspoon rice flour
  • 1 Tablespoon water
    • Other
  • 3 Ripe Mangos

Instructions

  1. Rinse and soak rice for 4 hours
  2. Cook Rice
  3. While the rice is cooking, make the coconut syrup. Combine ingredients in a small pot. Cook it over medium heat, and as soon as it starts to bubble, turn off the heat and cover. Few minutes before the rice is done, reheat the syrup so it is steaming hot then keep covered. When the rice is done, immediately place the rice into a mixing bowl and immediately pour the syrup over the rice. Stir to break up any lumps, then cover with a lid, a plate, or aluminum foil and let sit for 20 minutes.
    4. For the salted coconut sauce:
  4. Stir the rice flour into the water until there are no more lumps. 1 teaspoon rice flour, 1 Tablespoon water Combine the rice flour slurry, coconut milk, and salt in a small pot. Cook over medium high heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture comes to a boil. Remove from heat and pour into a bowl. Let cool completely before serving.
    5. Assembly:
  5. Serve the rice beside sweet, fresh cut mangoes. Spoon some salted coconut sauce over the rice and sprinkle with mango.
